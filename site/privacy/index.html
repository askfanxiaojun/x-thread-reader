Privacy policy

Your thread data stays in your browser.

X Thread Reader is designed as a local-first browser extension. This page explains what the extension does, what data it touches, and what it intentionally does not collect.

Effective date: April 2, 2026 Applies to the extension and this website

Privacy overview

X Thread Reader extracts public thread content from the X/Twitter page that you are already viewing. The goal is to help you save that content as Markdown for your own reading, notes, or archives.

We do not operate a remote parsing service, do not require an account, and do not upload extracted thread content to our own server as part of normal use.

What the extension accesses

  • The currently active X/Twitter tab when you trigger extraction.
  • Visible thread content and image links needed to generate the Markdown export.
  • Your browser clipboard only when you choose the copy action.

What we do not collect

  • No account registration details.
  • No saved thread history on our servers.
  • No analytics tied to the content of the threads you export.
  • No sale of personal data.

How exports are handled

When you copy Markdown or download a `.md` file, that output is created locally in your browser session. Where the file goes after that depends on your own device, browser, or note-taking workflow.

Website analytics

This marketing site is currently built as a static site. If analytics are added later, they should be privacy-conscious, minimal, and avoid collecting the content of exported threads. If that changes, this page should be updated.

Changes to this policy

If the extension adds a cloud feature, account system, or any server-side processing in the future, this privacy policy must be updated before those changes go live.

Quick facts

  • Local-only extraction
  • No required sign-in
  • No third-party thread storage
  • Markdown export generated client-side
